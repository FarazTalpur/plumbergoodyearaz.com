Frequently Asked Questions

A slow-draining bathroom sink is often caused by hair, soap scum, or other debris accumulating in the drain. Cleaning out the P-trap or using a plunger or a drain snake can help clear the blockage.

To fix a leaky bathroom faucet, first turn off the water supply. Then, disassemble the faucet to check if the washer, O-ring, or cartridge needs to be replaced. Reassemble the faucet after replacing the faulty parts.

A constantly running toilet can be due to a faulty flapper, an improperly calibrated float, or an old fill valve. Inspect these components and replace or adjust them as necessary to stop the running water.

To unclog a toilet without a plunger, you can use hot water and dish soap to help break down the clog. Pour a few cups of dish soap into the toilet, followed by hot water, and allow it to sit for 15-30 minutes before flushing.

Low water pressure in a shower can be caused by mineral deposits in the showerhead, a partially closed valve, or plumbing issues. Cleaning the showerhead with vinegar or checking that all valves are fully open can often resolve the issue.
